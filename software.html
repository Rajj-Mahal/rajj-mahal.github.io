How did I decide to be a software developer?

I spent the first ten years of my life in India, where access to technology was far more limited than what I later experienced in the United States. Despite that, I was always drawn to technology. I loved the digital world because it felt limitless—where imagination could become reality, whether that meant exploring virtual worlds or taking on challenges I could never face in real life.
At the same time, I was equally drawn to art. I’ve always had a strong connection to creativity, color, and design, just as much as I did to devices, cables, and screens.
Fast forward fifteen years, and choosing a career path became an opportunity to combine those passions. Software development allows me to merge creativity with functionality—to build experiences that are both visually engaging and technically sound. For me, it was a natural and clear decision.

and more importantly WHY?

One of my first experiences with software development was helping a small family-owned business build their first website as part of a school project. The site allowed them to move from manual record-keeping to an online system, making their data easier to manage while also expanding their reach to a broader audience.
That experience showed me how important technology is for small businesses. As the world continues to move online, many of these businesses struggle to keep up—not because they lack passion or value, but because they lack access to technical resources.
As a software developer, I want to help close that gap. My goal is to give small businesses, creators, and communities the tools they need to grow, share their stories, and compete in an increasingly digital world.
